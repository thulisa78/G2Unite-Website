History of Gwili Gwili and the Formation of G2 Unite

Gwili Gwili began as a small grassroots movement rooted in community upliftment and local empowerment. Originating from a desire to create meaningful change in underdeveloped areas, the initiative was born from conversations, shared struggles, and a united vision of building better futures—particularly for young people. As the need for structured development became more evident, the founding members recognized that action had to go beyond conversation. This led to the birth of G2 Unite—a dynamic organisation formed with the mission to bridge the gap of development across communities. "G2" stands for Gwili Gwili, representing its origins, and "Unite" reflects the organisation’s core principle: unity through action. Since its inception, G2 Unite has launched a variety of tailor-made programmes designed to engage all age groups. The organisation places a strong emphasis on Youth Development and Enhancement, aiming to empower young people through education, mentorship, leadership training, and community involvement. At the same time, G2 Unite recognises the value of intergenerational connection. To this end, special programmes have been developed that bring together elders and youth, encouraging the transfer of wisdom, tradition, and life skills. Operating throughout the year, G2 Unite’s diverse initiatives are designed to create lasting impact, promote active citizenship, and nurture leaders who are ready to serve their communities with integrity and purpose.




G2 UNITE Founder

G2 Unite is an organization founded by Mhlawuleli Malusi, established to bridge the gap in community development. We offer a wide range of programs that run throughout the year, each tailored to address the needs of various age groups.

A significant focus of our work is on Youth Development and Empowerment. Our initiatives are designed to equip young people with the skills, knowledge, and support they need to thrive. Additionally, we have created intergenerational programs that actively involve elders, fostering collaboration and mutual learning between generations.
